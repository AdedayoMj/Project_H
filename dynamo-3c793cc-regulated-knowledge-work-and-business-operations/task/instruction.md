A corporate travel-and-expense (T&E) team needs one executive's international trip closed out against the written expense policy, then trimmed to the trip's reimbursement budget. `/app/input/itinerary.json` lists each calendar day of the trip with the city the traveler slept in that night (`sleeping_city`) and whether it was a personal (non-business) day. `/app/input/policy.json` gives every numeric parameter this task uses: the rounding mode, the per-diem first/last-day proration fraction, the solo and per-attendee client-meal caps, the max reimbursable foreign-transaction fee, the per-line and per-category-aggregate approval thresholds, the flat commute deduction for mileage, and the whole-trip reimbursement budget cap (`trip_reimbursement_cap_cents`). `/app/input/per_diem_rates.json` gives the daily meals-and-incidentals per-diem rate by city. `/app/input/expense_lines.csv` lists every submitted expense, one row per line, with columns `line_id, date, statement_post_date, category (MEALS/AIRFARE/MILEAGE), vendor, country, amount, transaction_currency, card_billing_currency, foreign_fee_amount, fee_free_alternative_existed, attendees, payer, origin_city, destination_city, is_personal_expense, priority`. `attendees` is a `;`-separated list of `Name:TYPE` (`TYPE` is `CLIENT` or `INTERNAL`), empty for a solo meal. `priority` is an integer 1–5 where 1 is the highest business priority. Fields irrelevant to a line's category are left blank (for example, `MILEAGE` rows leave the amount/currency/attendee fields blank). `/app/input/fx_rates.csv` gives, for a `(date, from_currency, to_currency)` key, the rate to convert one unit of `from_currency` into `to_currency`; same-currency conversions need no lookup. `/app/input/vat_reclaim_table.json` gives, per country, the VAT rate and the categories eligible for VAT reclaim there. `/app/input/mileage_rates.json` gives home-currency cents-per-mile by effective-date range. `/app/input/distance_matrix.csv` gives directional mileage between city pairs. `/app/input/counterfactual_fares.json` gives a single supplied business-only comparison airfare.

Compute each line's reimbursable amount and disposition in the following order.

**Exclusions first.** A line with `is_personal_expense` equal to `TRUE` is `NON_REIMBURSABLE` (reimbursable amount 0). A `MEALS` line whose `date` falls on a personal day in `itinerary.json` is also `NON_REIMBURSABLE` — unless it is a genuine client meal (at least one attendee is `CLIENT` and `payer` is `EXECUTIVE`), which stays reimbursable as business development. Then, a line whose `(vendor, amount, date, category)` exactly matches an earlier still-eligible line's is `DUPLICATE_EXCLUDED` (reimbursable amount 0) — the first occurrence in the file is kept, not excluded.

**Per-line amount** (for lines that survive the exclusions above). For every `MEALS` or `AIRFARE` line: if the line's `country` and `category` are VAT-reclaim-eligible, subtract that country's VAT rate from `amount` (rounded to the nearest cent, ties rounding up) before anything else. Convert the remaining amount to the home currency in two dated hops: `transaction_currency` to `card_billing_currency` using the `fx_rates.csv` rate for `date`, then `card_billing_currency` to home currency using the rate for `statement_post_date`; round to the nearest cent after each hop. For `MEALS`, the per-attendee blended cap (the cap-per-head figure times headcount, where headcount is the number of names listed in `attendees` — the executive is not itself listed there) applies only when at least one attendee is `CLIENT` and `payer` is `EXECUTIVE`; every other meal (solo, or an all-`INTERNAL` group) is capped at the flat solo amount regardless of headcount. For `AIRFARE`, if any day in `itinerary.json` is a personal day, the reimbursable amount is the lesser of the converted actual fare and `counterfactual_fares.json`'s fare, compared per `AIRFARE` line independently; otherwise the full converted fare applies. If `transaction_currency` differs from `card_billing_currency` and `fee_free_alternative_existed` is not `TRUE`, convert `foreign_fee_amount` through the same two hops and add it to the line's reimbursement, capped at the policy's max fee. `MILEAGE` lines skip VAT and currency conversion entirely: look up the directional mileage for `(origin_city, destination_city)`, multiply by the rate in effect on `date`, subtract the flat commute deduction, and floor the result at zero.

**Per diem, net of meals.** Personal days pay nothing. For every other day, look up that night's `sleeping_city` rate; if that day is also the trip's first or last calendar day (by date), pay the policy's proration fraction of that rate instead of the full rate. Then, because per diem already covers meals, reduce each day's per-diem by the total reimbursable amount of the `MEALS` lines dated that day (using each meal's per-line amount computed above, before any budget deferral; excluded meals contribute nothing), floored at zero for that day. Sum across all days for `total_per_diem_cents`.

**Approval flags.** Walk each category's surviving lines in ascending `(date, line_id)` order, keeping a running total of their per-line amounts; a line is flagged for manager approval if its own amount exceeds the per-line threshold, or if the running total through that line exceeds that category's aggregate threshold (this can flag a line whose own amount is small).

**Trip budget.** The trip's total payout is `total_per_diem_cents` plus the reimbursable amount of every surviving line; call the untrimmed figure `gross_reimbursable_cents`. If it exceeds `trip_reimbursement_cap_cents`, defer whole lines (never per diem, never a partial line) until the payout fits the cap, choosing which lines to keep so as to maximize, in strict priority order: (1) the total priority weight retained, where a line of priority `p` contributes weight `6 − p`; then (2) the total retained line amount; then (3) break remaining ties by walking the surviving lines in ascending `(date, line_id)` order and keeping each line whenever keeping it is still consistent with (1), (2), and the cap — a unique selection. A deferred line's status is `DEFERRED_OVER_BUDGET` with reimbursable amount 0. Every kept line kept its earlier flag: `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` if it was flagged above, otherwise `APPROVED`.

Do not modify anything under `/app/input/`. Write `/app/output.json`, a single JSON object with exactly two keys. `line_items`: an array with one entry per row of `expense_lines.csv`, each `{"line_id": ..., "reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "status": "APPROVED"|"NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL"|"DUPLICATE_EXCLUDED"|"NON_REIMBURSABLE"|"DEFERRED_OVER_BUDGET"}`. `trip_summary`: `{"total_per_diem_cents": <integer>, "gross_reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "trip_budget_cap_cents": <integer>, "final_reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "deferred_over_budget_line_ids": [<line_id>, ...], "total_flagged_for_approval_cents": <integer>, "category_breakdown": {"MEALS": {...}, "AIRFARE": {...}, "MILEAGE": {...}}}`. `gross_reimbursable_cents` is `total_per_diem_cents` plus every surviving line's per-line amount before budget deferral; `final_reimbursable_cents` is `total_per_diem_cents` plus every kept (non-deferred) line's amount, and never exceeds the cap. `deferred_over_budget_line_ids` is the sorted list of deferred `line_id`s. Each category entry is `{"reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "flagged_for_approval_cents": <integer>}` summed over that category's kept `APPROVED` and `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` lines only (a `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` line's amount counts toward both fields; deferred and excluded lines count toward neither); `total_flagged_for_approval_cents` sums only kept `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` lines. All monetary values are integer home-currency cents, never a string or a decimal.
