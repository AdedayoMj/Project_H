A corporate travel-and-expense (T&E) team needs one executive's international trip closed out against the written expense policy. `/app/input/itinerary.json` lists each calendar day of the trip with the city the traveler slept in that night (`sleeping_city`) and whether it was a personal (non-business) day. `/app/input/policy.json` gives every numeric parameter this task uses: the rounding mode, the per-diem first/last-day proration fraction, the solo and per-attendee client-meal caps, the max reimbursable foreign-transaction fee, the per-line and per-category-aggregate approval thresholds, and the flat commute deduction for mileage. `/app/input/per_diem_rates.json` gives the daily meals-and-incidentals per-diem rate by city. `/app/input/expense_lines.csv` lists every submitted expense, one row per line, with columns `line_id, date, statement_post_date, category (MEALS/AIRFARE/MILEAGE), vendor, country, amount, transaction_currency, card_billing_currency, foreign_fee_amount, fee_free_alternative_existed, attendees, payer, origin_city, destination_city, is_personal_expense`. `attendees` is a `;`-separated list of `Name:TYPE` (`TYPE` is `CLIENT` or `INTERNAL`), empty for a solo meal. Fields irrelevant to a line's category are left blank (for example, `MILEAGE` rows leave the amount/currency/attendee fields blank). `/app/input/fx_rates.csv` gives, for a `(date, from_currency, to_currency)` key, the rate to convert one unit of `from_currency` into `to_currency`; same-currency conversions need no lookup. `/app/input/vat_reclaim_table.json` gives, per country, the VAT rate and the categories eligible for VAT reclaim there. `/app/input/mileage_rates.json` gives home-currency cents-per-mile by effective-date range. `/app/input/distance_matrix.csv` gives directional mileage between city pairs. `/app/input/counterfactual_fares.json` gives a single supplied business-only comparison airfare.

Per diem: for every non-personal day, look up that night's `sleeping_city` rate; the trip's first and last calendar day (by date, regardless of personal-day status) are paid at the policy's proration fraction of the full daily rate; personal days pay nothing. Sum across all days.

For every `MEALS` or `AIRFARE` line: if the line's `country` and `category` are VAT-reclaim-eligible, subtract that country's VAT rate from `amount` (rounded to the nearest cent, ties rounding up) before anything else. Convert the remaining amount to the home currency in two dated hops: `transaction_currency` to `card_billing_currency` using the `fx_rates.csv` rate for `date`, then `card_billing_currency` to home currency using the rate for `statement_post_date`; round to the nearest cent after each hop. For `MEALS`, the per-attendee blended cap (the cap-per-head figure times headcount) applies only when at least one attendee is `CLIENT` and `payer` is `EXECUTIVE`; every other meal (solo, or an all-`INTERNAL` group) is capped at the flat solo amount regardless of headcount. For `AIRFARE`, if any day in `itinerary.json` is a personal day, the reimbursable amount is the lesser of the converted actual fare and `counterfactual_fares.json`'s fare; otherwise the full converted fare applies. If `transaction_currency` differs from `card_billing_currency` and `fee_free_alternative_existed` is not `TRUE`, convert `foreign_fee_amount` through the same two hops and add it to the line's reimbursement, capped at the policy's max fee.

`MILEAGE` lines skip VAT and currency conversion entirely: look up the directional mileage for `(origin_city, destination_city)`, multiply by the rate in effect on `date`, subtract the flat commute deduction, and floor the result at zero.

Disposition precedence, in order: a line with `is_personal_expense` equal to `TRUE` is `NON_REIMBURSABLE` (reimbursable amount 0), regardless of any other rule. Otherwise, a line whose `(vendor, amount, date, category)` exactly matches an earlier line's is `DUPLICATE_EXCLUDED` (reimbursable amount 0) — the first occurrence in the file is kept, not excluded. Otherwise, walk each category's remaining lines in ascending `(date, line_id)` order, keeping a running total of their computed reimbursable amounts; a line is `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` if its own computed amount exceeds the per-line threshold, or if the running total through that line exceeds that category's aggregate threshold — this can flag a line even though its own amount is small. Every remaining line is `APPROVED`.

Do not modify anything under `/app/input/`. Write `/app/output.json`, a single JSON object with exactly two keys. `line_items`: an array with one entry per row of `expense_lines.csv`, each `{"line_id": ..., "reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "status": "APPROVED"|"NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL"|"DUPLICATE_EXCLUDED"|"NON_REIMBURSABLE"}`. `trip_summary`: `{"total_reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "total_flagged_for_approval_cents": <integer>, "total_per_diem_cents": <integer>, "category_breakdown": {"MEALS": {...}, "AIRFARE": {...}, "MILEAGE": {...}}}`, where each category's entry is `{"reimbursable_cents": <integer>, "flagged_for_approval_cents": <integer>}` summed over that category's `APPROVED` and `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` lines (a `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` line's amount counts toward both fields). `total_reimbursable_cents` is the sum of every line's `reimbursable_cents` plus `total_per_diem_cents`; `total_flagged_for_approval_cents` sums only `NEEDS_MANAGER_APPROVAL` lines. All monetary values are integer home-currency cents, never a string or a decimal.
